About Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the first manned mission to land on the Moon. Launched by NASA on July 16, 1969, it marked one of humanity’s greatest scientific and exploratory achievements.

The Crew

  • Neil Armstrong – Mission Commander and first man to walk on the Moon.
  • Buzz Aldrin – Lunar Module Pilot, second man to walk on the Moon.
  • Michael Collins – Command Module Pilot who remained in lunar orbit.

The Mission Plan

Apollo 11's main objective was to perform a crewed lunar landing and return safely to Earth. It consisted of the launch, lunar orbit insertion, moon landing, moonwalks, and Earth re-entry.

Historic Moon Landing

On July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin landed the Lunar Module “Eagle” on the Moon’s surface. Armstrong's famous words, “That’s one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind,” echoed across the world.

Global Impact

  • Technological advancements in computing, navigation, and aerospace.
  • Inspired generations in science, technology, and exploration.
  • Boosted national pride and space race victory for the U.S.
  • Strengthened global interest in astronomy and Earth sciences.
